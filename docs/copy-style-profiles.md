# Copy Style Profiles

Use these profiles when drafting Brevo drip campaign copy, campaign QA recommendations, or marketing message variants.

The profile shapes copy only. It does not change the no-direct-send boundary: Codex drafts and reviews; the user sends, schedules, or activates inside Brevo.

## Profile Matrix

| Profile | Best for | Voice | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Warm Professional | Services, consultants, local businesses | Clear, helpful, direct, lightly personal | Hype, jokes, dense jargon |
| Expert Advisor | Technical, B2B, high-consideration offers | Specific, evidence-led, calm | Overpromising, vague claims |
| Friendly Local | Community businesses, home services, appointment-based offers | Neighborly, concise, reassuring | Excessive informality, pressure |
| Premium Concise | High-ticket offers, design, professional services | Polished, spare, confident | Long explanations, discount-first framing |
| Recovery And Re-Engagement | Winback, inactive subscribers, abandoned inquiry | Respectful, low-pressure, clear options | Guilt, urgency tricks, repeated asks |

## Applying A Profile

For each campaign brief, state:

- Selected profile.
- Why it fits the audience and trigger.
- Subject-line style.
- CTA style.
- Words or claims to avoid.
- Example personalization fallback tone.

## Subject And Preheader Defaults

- Warm Professional: plain-language benefit plus helpful next step.
- Expert Advisor: specific insight or decision point.
- Friendly Local: approachable, concrete, lightly personal.
- Premium Concise: short, restrained, outcome-oriented.
- Recovery And Re-Engagement: respectful choice or status check.

## CTA Defaults

- Warm Professional: `Book a consultation`, `See the checklist`, `Reply with a question`.
- Expert Advisor: `Review the recommendation`, `Compare the options`, `See the next step`.
- Friendly Local: `Schedule a time`, `Ask us a question`, `Get help choosing`.
- Premium Concise: `Review the proposal`, `Reserve a time`, `Confirm next steps`.
- Recovery And Re-Engagement: `Update your preferences`, `Still interested?`, `Close this request`.

## Safety Rules

- Avoid false urgency.
- Avoid claims that cannot be substantiated.
- Avoid implying the user has taken an action that is only inferred.
- Keep personalization fallbacks natural.
- Keep unsubscribe and preference-management language clear.
