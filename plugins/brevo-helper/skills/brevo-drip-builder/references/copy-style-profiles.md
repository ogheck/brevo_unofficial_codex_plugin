# Brevo Copy Style Profiles

Use these profiles to keep Brevo drip campaign copy consistent across projects. Adapt them to the user's actual brand guidance when available.

## Profile Matrix

| Profile | Best for | Voice | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Warm Professional | Services, consultants, local businesses | Clear, helpful, direct, lightly personal | Hype, jokes, dense jargon |
| Expert Advisor | Technical, B2B, high-consideration offers | Specific, evidence-led, calm | Overpromising, vague claims |
| Friendly Local | Community businesses, home services, appointment-based offers | Neighborly, concise, reassuring | Excessive informality, pressure |
| Premium Concise | High-ticket offers, design, professional services | Polished, spare, confident | Long explanations, discount-first framing |
| Recovery And Re-Engagement | Winback, inactive subscribers, abandoned inquiry | Respectful, low-pressure, clear options | Guilt, urgency tricks, repeated asks |

## Universal Copy Rules

- Keep one primary CTA per message.
- Make the first sentence match the trigger that caused the email.
- Use personalization only when the fallback still reads naturally.
- Keep subject lines specific and honest.
- Keep preheaders complementary instead of repetitive.
- Include a plain-text-friendly version of the core message.
- Do not make guarantees the business cannot substantiate.

## Output Fields

When applying a profile, include:

- Selected profile.
- Why it fits the audience and trigger.
- Subject-line style.
- CTA style.
- Words or claims to avoid.
- Example fallback tone for personalization.

## Default Choice

If the user gives no voice guidance, use Warm Professional unless the campaign clearly fits another profile.
